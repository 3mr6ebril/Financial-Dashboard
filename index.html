📖 How Dutch Tax Works ▼
Box 1 — Work & Home

Covers salary, freelance income, and business profit. Income tax is progressive: 35.82% up to ~€38k, 37.48% up to ~€76k, 49.50% above.

Two key credits reduce your bill: the earned income credit (phases in then out at higher incomes, up to ~€5,247 in 2025) and the general tax credit (applied to all taxpayers, up to ~€3,068).

If you are a self-employed entrepreneur (ZZP), the self-employed deduction and SME profit exemption further reduce taxable profit before brackets apply.

Box 3 — Savings & Investments

Taxes a deemed return on net wealth above the tax-free allowance (€57,684 in 2025). Savings accounts are taxed at a flat ~1.37%; investments at ~5.88%. No tax on actual realised gains.

The reference date (peildatum) is always 1 January. Wealth on that date determines your annual Box 3 bill — mid-year changes don't matter.

Foreign dividend withholding tax withheld abroad (shown in Dividend Withholding below) is credited against Box 3 liability, often reducing it to zero.

Dividend Withholding

NL dividends: Dutch companies withhold 15% dividend tax at source. This is fully creditable against your income tax bill in Box 1.

Foreign dividends: rate depends on tax treaty. US ETFs typically withhold 15% (or 30% without treaty). Excess WHT beyond what you owe in Box 3 may be partially refundable.

Enter NL dividend tax amounts via ✏️ Edit record → the dashboard credits it against your Box 1 summary automatically.

📋 How to use this sheet
  1. Set your income + deductions in Tax Settings.
  2. After filing, click ✏️ Edit record on the year pill to store actual numbers from your employer statement and tax assessment.
  3. The Tax Year Summary shows the current estimate. The Filed vs Dashboard card (below Tax Year Summary) compares it against your actual filed numbers.
  4. Box 3 wealth is assessed on 1 Jan — update prices before that date for an accurate snapshot.
  5. Use ✏️ Edit record to note your provisional assessment amount and payment date.
